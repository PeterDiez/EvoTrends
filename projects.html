Case 01
Full Commercialization Partner
FIDCHEM
From lab-proven chemistry to international market presence
DeepTech Environmental Chemistry Horizon Europe
Challenge

FIDCHEM had developed scientifically proven deep-tech environmental chemistry solutions with genuine innovation — but possessed zero commercial structure. No market definition existed, no competitive landscape analysis, no understanding of who would actually buy the technology or why, and no funding roadmap. The core problem: industrial and environmental chemistry is a high-barrier sector requiring regulatory credibility, domain expertise, and established relationships within tightly-networked supply chains and procurement processes.

Our Role

EvoTrends became the full commercialization partner, building the entire commercial infrastructure from first principles. This included designing market positioning and value proposition architecture, structuring EU funding strategy (Horizon Europe program alignment), building investor narratives and fundraising preparation, and managing international market entry across industrial partners and public sector procurement channels in the EU and CIS regions.

Scope of Work
› Market positioning & value proposition design
› Competitive landscape & market definition
› EU funding strategy & Horizon Europe program alignment
› Investor narrative & fundraising preparation
› International market entry (EU + industrial partners)
› Technology communication for non-technical audiences
› Product & technology positioning strategy

Case 02
Co-founder & Product Lead
Resto-AI
Market research-driven pivot from waste reduction to AI platform consolidation
AI / SaaS Market Research Product Pivot Co-founded
Challenge & Pivot

Initial concept: a food waste reduction system for restaurants. After deep market research across hundreds of restaurants mapping operational pain points, decision-making patterns, and willingness-to-pay, we discovered the core insight — restaurants had zero demand for waste reduction as a standalone product. This research-driven realization led to a dramatic pivot: instead of a niche waste tool, we repositioned the platform as an AI-powered consolidation hub integrating all existing restaurant management systems (POS, reservations, inventory, delivery apps) into a unified operational intelligence platform.

Our Role

As co-founder, led the full product and market development: conducted extensive customer discovery across the restaurant industry, managed the strategic pivot based on market signals, defined AI product strategy and feature architecture, designed business model and pricing, recruited domain experts and external consultants on equity basis to build specialized knowledge without burning runway, and executed launch through a startup accelerator program. The entire approach centered on letting the market data guide product definition before heavy development investment.

Scope of Work
› Deep market research across restaurant operator networks
› Customer discovery & willingness-to-pay analysis
› Strategic product pivot based on market insights
› AI platform strategy & feature definition
› Business model & pricing architecture
› Startup accelerator program participation
› Expert recruitment & external consultant network building

Key lesson: Deep customer discovery before product build prevents expensive misdirection. Willingness-to-pay research revealed we were building for the wrong problem — the pivot toward consolidation and platform integration reflected actual restaurant operator priorities and created a defensible market opportunity.

Case 03
Market Entry & Commercial Development Lead
International Market Entry
Building commercial infrastructure in complex B2B verticals
Market Entry B2B CIS Region Caribbean Basin
Challenge

Entering markets in the CIS region and Caribbean basin where standard go-to-market playbooks fail entirely. Commercial relationships in these environments are built on personal trust, unwritten rules, multi-tier distribution networks, and long-term relationship cultivation — not digital marketing or transactional sales. Standard Western B2B commercial strategies are ineffective because the underlying operating assumptions are fundamentally different: decision-making is opaque, trust-based, and relationship-dependent. Technology and hospitality companies scaling internationally needed partners who understood both the market dynamics and the operational complexities of building distribution in these environments.

Our Role

EvoTrends built and managed the full commercial infrastructure for technology and hospitality clients entering CIS and Caribbean markets. This meant developing partner networks from scratch (100+ B2B partners across CIS), executing in-market representation and strategy, managing industry event participation, establishing direct channels with major regional operators, and maintaining relationships through economic disruption. We acted as the commercial bridge between Western technology companies and complex regional markets, translating both product value propositions and business models to operate within local commercial ecosystems.

Scope of Work
› Partner network development (100+ B2B partners across CIS)
› In-market roadshows & industry presentations
› Trade event representation across Europe, Asia & Caribbean
› Direct channel development with major operators (TUI, Coral Travel, ANEX)
› Crisis-period market maintenance (COVID-era 2020: zero client losses)
› Commercial strategy & relationship management

Pattern recognition from this work directly informs our DeepTech commercialization approach — the same principles apply: trust-first partner development, structured channel strategy, and long-term value positioning over transactional selling. High-barrier B2B sectors (industrial chemistry, advanced technology, regional hospitality) operate on similar relationship-centric models.
