Expertise

Core Competencies

Built across 20 years of operating in complex international environments — from hardware and product development to funding strategy and market entry. The through-line: structured thinking in non-obvious markets.

01
Venture Growth & Business Tracking

Structured support for early-stage and R&D-driven companies — growth strategy, KPI architecture, milestone planning, and investor readiness. Built from direct experience co-founding Resto-AI (where we ran the full product-market cycle including a major strategic pivot) and advising companies like FIDCHEM through commercialization from zero. We know what structured growth looks like from the inside — not just from advisory distance.

02
Scientific Approach to Market Entry

Market entry treated like a scientific experiment: hypotheses, validation tests, results, iterations. This methodology was developed through real market entry projects in environments where assumptions fail constantly — the CIS region, Caribbean basin, and Southeast Asia — markets where standard go-to-market playbooks break down immediately. We apply the same rigor to DeepTech commercialization. In the Resto-AI project, this meant abandoning a waste-reduction hypothesis after customer discovery and pivoting to a software integration platform. That intellectual honesty is rare. It saves companies years.

03
DeepTech Commercialization

Bridging science and business through structured strategy. DeepTech is different: longer cycles, higher capital needs, complex stakeholder ecosystems (labs, regulators, industrial clients), and the constant challenge of communicating technical value to non-technical audiences. We've worked hands-on with deep-tech hardware — interactive systems, robotics, CNC, 3D fabrication — and we've built commercialization strategies for environmental chemistry (FIDCHEM), AI products (Resto-AI), and education technology. We understand both ends of the conversation.

04
Complex Systems Thinking

Operating in multi-layered environments where technology, funding, regulation, and market dynamics move simultaneously. Real example: in complex international B2B markets, a pricing decision affects partner trust, which affects distribution reach, which affects revenue projections, which affects funding narratives. We hold all threads simultaneously. This is the skill that makes commercialization work — and it's built over 20 years of operating in environments that don't forgive linear thinking.
